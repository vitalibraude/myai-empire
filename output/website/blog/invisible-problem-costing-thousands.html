Your website looks fine to you. It loads, the pages are there, the phone number is right. So why aren't customers calling?

Because your website has problems you literally cannot see with your eyes. They're invisible to you — but not to Google, and not to your customers' browsers.

£8,400

average annual revenue lost by UK small businesses due to invisible website problems

The 5 Invisible Problems

Problem 1

Slow Load Speed

You're on fast broadband at your office, so your site feels fine. But your customers are on 4G, on a bus, loading your site alongside 20 other tabs. If your site takes more than 3 seconds, 53% of them leave. You'll never know — they just disappear.

Cost: ~£200-500/month in lost leads
Problem 2

Missing or Wrong Meta Tags

Meta titles and descriptions tell Google what your pages are about. If they're missing, duplicated, or generic ("Home" instead of "Emergency Plumber in Leeds — 24/7 Call-Out"), Google can't rank you properly. And the description users see in search results is wrong or auto-generated gibberish.

Cost: ~£300-800/month in invisible Google traffic
Problem 3

Broken Mobile Experience

Your site looks great on your laptop. But 61% of your visitors are on phones. If buttons are too small, text is unreadable, or forms don't work on mobile — they're gone. Google also ranks mobile-unfriendly sites lower since 2019.

Cost: ~£200-600/month in mobile customers lost
Problem 4

No SSL Certificate (HTTPS)

If your URL starts with "http://" instead of "https://", Chrome shows a "Not Secure" warning. 84% of users would abandon a purchase on an insecure site. Google also penalises non-HTTPS sites in rankings.

Cost: ~£100-300/month in scared-off visitors
Problem 5

No Structured Data

Structured data tells Google your reviews, prices, hours, and FAQ answers. Without it, your search listing is plain text while competitors show star ratings, prices, and rich snippets. They get the click. You don't.

Cost: ~£100-400/month in click-through rate lost
